From VHS to DVD!

VHS was the primary format of video from the 80's up until the late 90's. Some movies and shows were still being manufactured in the early 2000's. VHS was in a war we now look back on as the VHS vs Betamax war. VHS was ultimately the winner for a variety of reasons. VHS also had much higher quality audio and video output compared the prior formats such as Laserdisc.



!!RECORDING!!

One other big thing that ultimately made VHS so popular was it's ability to record anything off TV. During the 90's, all you needed was a VCR and a blank tape and you could record your favorite show, movie, or even the football game to watch later.


The Rise of DVD


Ultimately, VHS was going to be obsolete. Once DVD came around, there was simply no substitute for the time. This format offered VASTLY different visual and sound quality when compared to it's former format
